    3D Printing

    My classroom has four 3D printers which are utilized around the clock. The picture shown is an example of the wood filament that we use. The Apple watch charger shown was printed with wood filament, then sanded and stained. The printers also print in carbon fiber, stainless steel, and ABS plastic. My students learn how to print and maintain the 3D printers when they break.



    3D Modeling

    Every student in my classes learn how to model designs for the 3D printers. They use a CAD program called 123Design to create three dimensional designs. These designs range from custom robot parts, to phone cases, to the completely 3D printed tank you see below. My students are learning the basics for a career in any engineering field as 3D modeling is used from civil engineering to biomedical engineering.



    Advanced Robotics

    The tank shown to the left is a custom designed tank that was the senior project for one of my AP computer science students. In addition, the student made it remote controlled using an Arduino and a PS4 remote. The tank drives on its tracks, the turret moves, and the cannon shoots 6mm BB’s using an airsoft air system. The student coded the entire tank from scratch and continues to refine the design and programming.



    Robotics Club

    The Excelsior Academy Robotics club has 21 members who meet twice a week to compete in FTC (First Tech Challenge). This robot uses the Android operating system to communicate with PS3 remotes. This year’s challenge was to create a robot that can pick up whiffle balls, and shoot them into a hoop. In addition, the robot needed to be able to hit beacon buttons on the side wall. The robot to your right is the final product we brought to the competition. Like the tank above the robot was not only built but fully coded by the students.



    Classroom Projection Project

    I taught inside the juvenile justice system in New York City for three years. During that time, I recognized a need for projection devices in the classroom as the students would destroy a SMART Board within a month and projectors lasted less time. I created a system that utilized an Apple TV which was enclosed inside of a custom-built box that was covered in bullet proof glass. The result, shown to the left, was installed in all 100 classrooms within the school. They are still in use today and after three years only one TV has been broken by a student.



    Networking / System Administrator

    While working as a technology coach in the New York City Department of Education I wired, installed, and maintained networks in seven different buildings around the five boroughs. I now use than knowledge to teach my students about networking and being a system administrator. My students go to their college classes with a basic understanding of how networks work and how to troubleshoot them.
